# 01 — Idea Brief

## Purpose

Capture the raw game idea in enough structured form to support Vision Definition. Not a commitment — an invitation to investigate.

## Logline

A basketball coaching roguelite where you build a roster and coaching toolkit across seasons, make live card-based crisis decisions during games, and shape a persistent coaching legacy through emergent league stories.

## Genre and Platform Assumptions

- Genre: Basketball Coaching Roguelite (secondary influences: deck building, auto battler, RPG)
- Platform: Desktop first (Steam), with mobile viability to validate later
- Session length hypothesis: 2-12 minute playable games plus between-game and off-season management layers

## Target Player Hypothesis

Players who enjoy sports strategy and management games, and who want dramatic decision moments rather than pure spreadsheet simulation. They want to feel like a high-agency coach/GM whose philosophy and reputation matter across seasons.

## Core Fantasy

You are the coach as the true protagonist: reading a live game, making pressure decisions with imperfect information, and building a long-term identity that outlives any single roster.

## Initial References

| Reference | Take | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Basketball manager sims | Long-term roster and season arc thinking | Low-drama menus that flatten game-day tension |
| Deckbuilding games | Hand economy and meaningful constrained choices | Excessive randomness that obscures player agency |
| Auto battlers | Build expression tested by simulation outcomes | Over-automation that removes intervention skill |
| Sports broadcast drama | Narrative stakes and rivalry framing | Scripted outcomes that reduce emergent storytelling |

## Known Constraints

1. Full rival-team possession simulation is out of scope; league simulation is output-driven.
2. Trades are not part of the design scope (movement is contracts, waivers, free agency).
3. Every game is played; blowouts are shortened but not skipped.
4. Several foundation systems remain undefined (reputation model, offensive scheme vocabulary, injury MVP, scouting knowledge numeric layer).

## Unknowns

1. Reputation scoring range, formula, and tier thresholds.
2. Offensive scheme taxonomy and weighting inputs used by possession generation.
3. Injury severity and games-missed model, including emergency signing flow.
4. Numeric representation of scouting knowledge for simulation scoring.
5. Final platform target split (desktop only vs desktop plus mobile).

## Open Questions

1. None blocking Vision Definition. Open questions are captured as risks and follow-on design tasks.
