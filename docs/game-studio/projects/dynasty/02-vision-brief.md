# 02 — Vision Brief

## Purpose

Lock the game's identity before design expands. This is the first formal approval gate. Everything downstream references this document.

## Vision Paragraph

Hoop Dynasty is a basketball coaching roguelite for strategy players who want the tension of live game-day decisions and the long arc of career building. Each season is a run where the roster naturally churns, but your coach identity, reputation, and legacy toolkit persist, so every hard choice compounds into future opportunities. The game stands out by combining an action-driven basketball simulation with card-based crisis interventions and emergent league storytelling centered on the coach as the persistent protagonist.

## Creative Pillars

<!-- 3–5 non-negotiable design principles. Each pillar should pass the test: "Does this decision strengthen or weaken pillar X?" -->
<!-- Format: Pillar name — one sentence definition. -->

1. Coach-as-protagonist persistence - The coach identity and reputation are the long-term character progression layer.
2. Crisis decision play under pressure - High-leverage interventions during live simulation are the signature skill expression.
3. Basketball simulation credibility - Systems should produce believable basketball outcomes, roles, and team behavior.
4. Roguelite season arc with natural churn - Seasons reset rosters through basketball logic while preserving meta progression.
5. Emergent storytelling through systems - Rivalries, relationships, and league dynamics generate stories without scripting outcomes.

## Target Player

- Who they are: Sports strategy and management players who enjoy systems-driven outcomes and replayable runs.
- What they want to feel: Smart, composed under pressure, and responsible for both immediate game outcomes and long-term legacy.
- What games they already love: Basketball manager/sim titles, deckbuilders with constrained choices, and systems-heavy roguelites.
- Why they would choose this: It uniquely combines live intervention coaching with long-run identity progression.

## Success Criteria

<!-- What does a successful version of this game look like? How would we know it worked? -->
<!-- Measurable or observable where possible. -->

1. Players can explain the core loop as live game intervention plus between-game and off-season management.
2. A full season produces distinct narrative outcomes (relationships, rivalries, career offers) from system interactions.
3. Decision pressure feels meaningful in-game and between games without menu overload.
4. Replayability comes from changing rosters, evolving coach identity loadouts, and divergent league trajectories.

## Non-Goals

<!-- What this game deliberately is not. What it will not try to be. -->
<!-- These are as important as the pillars. -->

1. Full rival-team possession simulation is not a target.
2. Trade systems are not in scope.
3. Auto-resolve or skip-all gameplay loops are not the primary intended experience.
4. Rich narrative scripting systems are deferred in favor of emergent simulation first.

## Core Trade-Off Priorities

<!-- When pillars conflict, which wins? Explicit priority ordering. -->
<!-- Example: "Challenge over accessibility. Atmosphere over performance. Replayability over content volume." -->

1. Decision tension over exhaustive control.
2. Emergent replayability over handcrafted content volume.
3. Basketball believability over arcade spectacle.
4. Scope focus over system completeness in v1.

## Open Questions

2. Offensive scheme vocabulary and fit scoring definitions.
3. Injury system MVP details and emergency signing behavior.
4. Scouting knowledge numeric representation for simulation use.
